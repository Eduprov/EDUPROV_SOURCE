B.Tech ECE

B.Tech Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) as the name suggests is a combination of different technologies where one deals with electronic circuits, devices, integrated circuits, transmitters and receivers that are used for communication. One will gain in-depth knowledge about the basic concepts and the theories of the electronic devices and communication technology and principle in this course.

In this field, engineers are working in a group to design, produce, fabricate, supervise and test the manufacturing of complex systems and product, electronic communication equipment and components which are used in various industries like Defense, Satellite Networks, Entertainment, etc. They work with microprocessors, telecommunications, radio, fiber optics, Television etc.

A student can specialize in the field of Radio-Television Systems, Digital System Design, Signal Processing, Wireless Communications, Optical Communications. You may study some of these also Principles of Control Systems, Principles of Communication Engineering, Basic Electricity and Electronics, Communication Skills and Communication Circuit.

Eligibility

Bachelor’s Degree: For the degree course in Electronics and Communication Engineering candidates must qualify Higher Secondary School Certificate (HSC) Examination or equivalent with physics, chemistry, and mathematics as a compulsory subject. They need to appear in various entrance exams like IIT Joint Entrance Examination, AIEEE, and Common Entrance Test at the state or central level.

Master’s Degree: For a Master degree, the candidate must have B. Tech. degree in telecommunication from All India Council of Technical Education with approved University. Another option to become an Electronics and communication Engineer candidate must pass the Associate Membership Examination of the Institute of Engineers (AMIE).

Scope

It represents two rapidly growing technology areas. Information Society is also progressing rapidly, therefore, the role of communications becomes more vital to increased Industry efficiency and competition. Electronics and Communication Engineers can look for jobs in State Government, Central Governments and their sponsored corporations in public sectors like MTNL, All India Radio, Post, and Telegraph Department, Indian Telephone Industries, National Physical Laboratories, Bharat Electronics Limited, Coordination Department and some other government undertakings. Private Companies offer excellent salary packages for experienced and skilled professionals.

Many job opportunities are available for electronic degree holders in government organizations like Steel Authority of India Limited, Bharat Electronics Limited, Indian Railways, Keltron, Civil Aviation Department, Indian Oil Corporation, Hindustan Aeronautics Limited, BSNL, MTNL, Indian Engineering service and so on.
