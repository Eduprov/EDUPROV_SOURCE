Overview

GATE exam also known as Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering is a national level entrance examination conducted by the Indian Institute of Sciences and also the seven IIT`s which are in Roorkee, Guwahati, Chennai, Kanpur, Mumbai, Delhi and Kharagpur on behalf of the National Co-ordination Board and Government of India. GATE Exam is mainly conducted for admissions in post-graduation or higher studies from top institutes which constitute the education board. It gets financial support from the Ministry of Human Resources and Development of India (MHRD) and various other government agencies. It is the most competitive exams in India. The exam is a computerized standard test conducted at national level with an objective of volunteering to pick the best students for various engineering and other science fields.

Eligibility Criteria

GATE exam does have eligibility criteria for every course and also has different set of entitlement for international students. The eligibility criteria in terms of age limit and number of attempts is given below:

  1. There is no specific age limit to appear for the exam. Candidates of any age can apply for the entrance exam.
  2. candidates who reside in India and outside India can apply for the exam.
  3. The exam is held once in a year and the scores are valid for 3 years since the exam date. Thus, the student once qualified need not try for another attempt.

GATE exam will not just be attended by Indian students but many students outside India in foreign countries also are eligible to write the exam. The eligibility criteria again differ for International students.

  1. Candidates with the nationality of Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, UAE can get admissions through this exam or jobs.
  2. The candidates must have completed their Bachelor`s degree in engineering or the same such as 10+2+4
  3. Candidates can choose to apply for MTech or PhD program offered by IIT`s or IISC.
  4. There is no specific age limit as such.
  5. The basic eligibility is that candidates must have completed their program with a duration of 3 years.
  6. Candidates who have completed their final year or yet to complete their final year can also attend the exam.
  1. The candidates those who have had a break or completed their degree in the year 2018, need to submit a certificate of their completion such as degree certificate, provisional certificate etc.
  2. For those candidates those who have completed their degree in 2021 need to submit a certificate of education by the institute or principal for the attainment of score card of GATE 2022 exam.
  1. The UGC and MHRD along with IIT's and IISC decided to declare that BDS and MPharm are also eligible to apply for the exam.
  2. GATE two-paper combination has been revised just like last year.
  3. Two new subjects have been introduced Geometrics engineering and Naval Architecture and Marine engineering (NM).
  4. The total subjects for GATE 2022 have increased up to 29.
  5. According to the change in 2022 students can opt for two different papers from each set of combination prescribed.

Firstly, GATE is conducted once in a year, students can attempt the exam once. The scores of an attempted exam can be valid for three years. Nonetheless, the student if scored well in first attempt need not make a second attempt.

GATE exam is attended by lakhs of students all over India and foreign countries. The exam is most awaited engineering exams. Students mostly think that this exam is only for MTech, well this exam has definitely various to offer the students. Including job opportunities abroad and PSU Public Sector Undertakings. Many students take up M.E or MTech in their respective fields followed by their under graduation. But there are top 5 branches which most of the students look to apply for:

  1. Computer science
  2. Mechanical engineering
  3. Electrical and electronics engineering
  4. Civil engineering
  5. Aerospace engineering

The main reason for this course's popularity is believed to be its career prospects. Other than these specializations other branches such as aeronautical engineering, electronics and telecommunication engineering has also gained massive prominence.

GATE exam results are out usually around 40 days from the exam date. GATE marks and scores are two different entities. GATE score is calculated by the exam conducting authorities after evaluating all the marks of the papers they have appeared for.

  1. GATE score is for a total of 1000.
  2. GATE marks are usually out of 100.
  3. AIR: GATE highest ranking students all around the world.
  4. Minimum marks/ qualifying marks for different categories such as general category, OBC, SC/ST, PWD.
  5. Total number of candidates appearing for the exam.

After students complete their exam, they would be looking forward for their GATE exam scores and marks, because GATE rank would be attained depending upon the GATE score. Based on their scores the exam conducting authority will release the GATE qualifying marks, rest of which students can either apply for admissions in IIT`s or IIM`s or get recruited in PSU. In order to get into these top tier institutes student, need to get very good marks in the exam followed by aiming high for ranks below 200. These scores are valid for 3 years.

Exam Pattern

GATE exam paper pattern is somehow very polished and proficient with a stellar way of better understanding as there are different papers and the varies with additional subjects. GATE exam will be conducted for 29 subjects which comprises of 65 subjects. Which will be multiple choice questions (MCQ`s), objective types and numerical answer type questions. The duration of the exam would be for 3 hours and students need to manage their time effectively and efficiently.

  1. Multiple Choice Questions MCQ's: MCQ's are for two marks. The questions will be having 4 choices out of which only one choice would be correct.
  2. Multiple Choice Questions: MSQ's carry 1 or 2 marks in each of the GATE paper. The question will be having 4 choices out of which 1 or 2 choices would be correct.
  3. Numerical Type Questions: NAQ's carry 1 or 2 marks in each of the GATE papers. For these types of questions, the answer is a number and choices would be given for this. The candidate needs to enter a number with the help of virtual keypad which will be displayed on the monitor. The answers can be in integers, decimals etc.
