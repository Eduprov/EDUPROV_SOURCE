B.Tech Aerospace Engineering

B.Tech Aerospace Engineering is a very popular and chosen stream in the engineering discipline. And is considered to be one of the toughest branches of engineering which has carved a special and unique niche among the aspiring candidates.

Aerospace Engineering is a very wide branch of science and technology that deals with designing, manufacturing and maintaining heavy flying objects even above the earth’s surface. It is majorly into manufacturing spacecraft, space shuttles, rockets, missile and other weapon system. A very wide aspect of aerospace engineering is concerned with mechanical engineering as it involves the manufacturing of huge machineries.

Carved with perfection in every angle, we tend to admire how space shuttles function and how celestially these rockets and missiles have been designed. However, all the omnificent and resilient job is done by aerospace engineers. All the hard work pays off as the course gives wings to your life and growth to your career.

Aerospace Engineering also covers the other two aspects of engineering namely Aeronautical Engineering and Astronomical Engineering. Aerospace engineering is one of the most on demand and challenging field in the engineering umbrella. The two overlapping branches are also important and have good scope. Aerospace Engineering is also given as a specialization in Mechanical engineering.

Designing of a flight or a space shuttle involves a lot of other engineering disciplines such as avionics engineering, mechanical, aerodynamics stability and control system etc. Aerospace Engineering deals with the usage of rocket engines, space shuttles and all heavy objects that fly above the earth’s surface. Aerospace Engineering is also an aviation related course.

B.Tech Aerospace Engineering is a 4-year under graduate course which is further divided into 8 semesters. Aerospace Engineering has gone to become quiet ostensible field and people tend to make use of aerospace every single day and specifically government and aviation companies depend upon aerospace for security purpose and production. Aerospace engineers have gone to leave a doting mark in aviation and technology in the realm of science and engineering principles.

Eligibility Criteria

  • The candidates must have completed their 10+2 from a recognized board or university.
  • They should have completed their 12th with a minimum of 60% in PCM.
  • The candidates must have completed their 12th with PCM as mandatory subjects.
  • For the reserved categories such as OBC, SC/ST or PWD the minimum eligibility will be 55%.
  • Diploma holders in aerospace engineering can also get into the B.Tech program through lateral entry admission.

Admission Process

The admission process for aerospace engineering is not at all a complicated process, it is just like any other engineering course. Admissions can happen in B.Tech aerospace engineering either through entrance exams or merit basis or one can get direct admission in college. Entrance exams are conducted state level, national level and university level. Candidates can attend any entrance exam depending upon which college they want to get into and score less ranking for government seats. Based on the performance of the candidate, colleges will prepare a merit list and will conduct counseling rounds and candidates have to qualify all around such as personal interview until the last round.

For direct admissions for Aerospace Engineering the candidate need not attend any entrance exams, they can just apply for the prescribed course that they want to choose through the institute’s official website. Universities will have set an eligibility criterion and the candidates need to fulfil that and pay the admission fee and secure the seat.

Course Curriculum

Diploma in Aerospace Engineering: Diploma in Aerospace engineering is for a tenure of 3 years and students can take up this course after completion of their 10th standard.

Undergraduate or degree program in B.Tech or B.E: B.Tech or B.E is a 4- year undergraduate degree course that students can take up after completion of 12th standard and is a very commonly opted course.

Post- graduation or M.E or M.Tech: M.E or M.Tech in aerospace engineering is the course that is usually considered as higher education and those interested in doing PG can do this after completion of their under- graduation program or B.Tech Aerospace Engineering.

Specialization

  • Aerodynamics

  • Navigation
  • Aeronautics
  • Aircraft and spacecraft guidance and control
  • Space mission design and planning
  • Space and atmospheric flight optimal control
  • Avionics
  • Spacecraft and vehicle design
  • Celestial mechanics
