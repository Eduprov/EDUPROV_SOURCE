B.Sc Operation Theatre Technology

Bachelor of science in operation theatre technology is commonly known as B.Sc in operation theatre technology is a three years long allied health sciences undergraduate degree that enables the student to work in the field of health care as an operation theatre technician. They are medical professionals that assist physicians during surgeries and invasive procedures. The operation theatre technicians during the B.Sc course have to learn both medical procedures in practicals along with their theory. The course also includes a six-month-long internship to get more hands-on experience. Bachelor of science in operation theatre technology is a well in demand course across the world. During the course of study, the future operational theatre technologists will learn how to care for patients during operations and surgery, how to assist surgeons and doctors during sergons, aftercare of patients in ICU etc.

Eligibility

Students interested in taking admissions to this course must have passed their three-year diploma in allied health sciences or paramedical sciences after their 10th standard. Or they must have finished their PUC or 12th standard's equivalent course with physics, chemistry and biology as their core subjects.

The students must have had a minimum of 45 per cent if they are from the general category or OBC category. However, students belonging to SC, ST, Category 1 and physically handicapped students need to have at least 40 per cent marks in their examinations for the admissions. They must also be over the age of seventeen when they fill out the application for this course.

Students can either apply for this course through Karnataka CET if they wish to get admissions to the course in top government medical colleges. The applications for BSc allied medical courses usually open in the month of August. But to get a seat in top private medical and paramedical colleges in India, students have to apply to the colleges directly. There are no entrance exams for all colleges but, JIPMER and CMC Vellore hold different entrance exams for the seats in their colleges. So, be sure to apply for the entrances in the month of January and February.

Scope

The completion of this course can lead to a job in Intensive care units, operation theatre, neonatal care units, trauma centres and other fields needing emergency and surgical care. They can find work in military hospitals, clinics, government hospitals, private hospitals etc Alternatively they can do their masters or higher education to become lecturers in paramedical colleges and medical colleges to teach operational technology and care.
