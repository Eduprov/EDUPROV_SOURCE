B.Tech Petroleum Engineering

Petroleum Engineering is a rare course and is not available in most of the engineering colleges Only few intakes are done every year. Since there will be only less number of candidates who will pass out every year the scope for this course is high and will not diminish in the future too. In case of learning it will explore you to a new world and will be adventurous. So choosing the course will be a wise choice.

Bachelor of Technology (B. Tech.) or Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Petroleumis an undergraduate course for the study of science and technologies related to extraction, production, refining and consumption of hydrocarbons (crude oil and natural gas). The course is offered in engineering colleges for the duration of four years which include a semester of internship as well. After completing this course successfully, the student is conferred B.Tech/B.E. in Petroleum and is mostly referred as a petroleum engineer.

The course subjects are essentially divided into three main divisions out of which the first one is mainly taught in the first year while the second and third category which cover the technical aspects are covered in subsequent years.

  • The basic fundamentals of engineering include Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, geology, thermodynamics, etc
  • The Upstream sector includes the exploration and production of petroleum and natural gas
  • The downstream Sector includes refining of the hydrocarbons and their marketing.

Eligibility

  • To be eligible for the course, one must have qualified 10+2 in the Non-Medical stream (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics).
  • State-level exams are also conducted for getting admissions into a state college

To get admission to an Engineering college, there are two main competitive exams held nationwide. JEE (Joint Entrance Exam) is conducted for getting admission to IIT’s. AIEEE (All India Engineering Entrance Exam) for getting admissions is NITs and other premier institutes of India.

Scope

Both the private and public sectors have a huge demand for petroleum engineers. They are employed as a reservoir engineer, Drilling Engineers, Safety inspectors, Officers in various Oil companies, Production engineers, etc. One can also opt for the teaching profession in college by pursuing a Masters's Degree or Ph.D. Petroleum Engineers enjoy a high demand overseas especially in the Gulf region and UAE.
