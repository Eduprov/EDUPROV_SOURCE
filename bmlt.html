Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT)

Course

Our body is a great composition of a variety of things. We always have an essential requirement of components like the proteins, carbohydrates, vitamins, and so on for nourishing our body, but when we ignore any of these things then what happens? We face the problem of health usually and in the ongoing world, the health problems are quite viral. In the moribund life, we all are so badly bound with our responsibilities that we are ignoring our health at every step of our life. For studying the essential requirement of our body and what are the things needed for it there is a course that people study and that is the Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Technology.

In normal terms, the study of the body at the molecular level lies under this course. People studying this course always deals with the DNA, RNA, Tissues, Cells, and other micro molecular things related to human life like for example if anyone is a patient of AIDS is there, then there is a test for it named ELISA, so when this test is conducted the blood sample of the patient is taken and it then goes to the Medical Laboratory, where it is found that whether the patient is really suffering from the disease or not. So, this laboratory work is either done by the students or the professionals, who perform this test, and apart from the disease study, there are other things like studying the genes, genetics related problems, fingerprinting, and much more. All these important tasks are accomplished by the students or the qualifiers, who study this course.

Eligibility

  • This course is done at both the levels, Graduate and Postgraduate and here are the few things that are required for this course
  • Minimum age for opting this course is 18 years old.
  • To pursue this course at the graduate level the student needs to have passed the High School and the Intermediate (Science stream, Biology) from a recognized board.
  • To pursue this course at the post-graduate level, one needs to have good aggregate from a recognized university in any of the biology courses.

Scope
