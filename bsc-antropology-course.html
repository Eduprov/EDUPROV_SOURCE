B.Sc Anthropology

Bachelor of Science in Anthropology is a three years long undergraduate degree that is highly in demand, interesting and helps you reach out to people and learn. This course is mainly targeted towards those students who are interested in studying a comparison based study on human similarities and differences, cultural and biological histories throughout the world, the evolution of people with culture, patterns of culture throughout the world, ideologies followed and used by people, major believes followed and practiced by people around the world, exploitation, and protection of natural history and natural resources by humans and related issues. This is one of the best mixtures of interdisciplinary studies that involve both science and humanities. So, if you are a student who has a passion for both science and humanities, this is the best course for you. Eligibility

  • The basic eligibility to join the Bachelor of Science in Anthropology course are as follows
  • Students need to have completed their 10+2 studies with physics, biology, chemistry, maths, and English as their core subjects.
  • Students must have secured at least a minimum of 45% in their 12th standard or PUC exams.

Scope

The completion of a BSc degree in Anthropology can help the students get a job as anthropologists, archaeologist, field technicians, field investigator, cultural anthropologist, research scientist, assistant professor, teacher, forensic anthropologist, conservation officer, historic building or monument inspector, curator, field assistant, government officer, etc. in several government and private organizations. Some of the popular job providers are WHO, UNICEF, UNESCO, Non-government organizations (NGOs), Not for Profit organizations (NPOs), State and central government, UPSC, Private technology companies, Information technology MNCs, Colleges and Universities, Archives departments, Museums, etc. where the fresh graduates can earn anywhere between two lakh rupees to six lakh rupees n the entry-level job. The fresh graduates are also eligible to write competitive exams like UPSC, RAS, IAS, etc for various state and central government jobs.
