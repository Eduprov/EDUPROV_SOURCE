B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering

Bachelor of Technology (B. Tech.) or Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Mechanical , mostly referred to as Mechanical Engineering is an undergraduate program offered at engineering colleges for the duration of four years. Mechanical engineers are responsible for designing, developing and researching new and improved machinery, mechanical equipment's and equipment. It is one of the oldest branches of engineering and has a history of thousands of years which includes the period of Archenemies.

It is the largest of all engineering disciplines covering extensive research on Machine design, material science thermodynamics, Auto CAD, heat transfer, fluid flow, and momentum transfer. This course has more scope around the world.

  • There are various state and nationwide competitive entrance exams conducted every year for getting admission into mechanical engineering.
  • The minimum eligibility criteria for getting into this course is 10+2 with Non-Medical stream, or one must have qualified with physics, chemistry, and mathematics with a minimum of 45% marks.

There are two main nationwide exams held in India. These are AIEEE (for NITs and other premium institutes) and JEE (for IITs). All admissions are made strictly on merit basis

Mechanical engineering offers a lot of job prospects almost in govt as well as the private sector. Small, Medium and Large scale industrial units hire several numbers of Mechanical Engineers to function powerfully. When it comes to the Government sector and organizations like- Indian Railways, Indian Armed Forces, State Wise Road Transport boards, etc recruits Mechanical Engineers in bulk. The automobile sector and oil companies are the major recruiters. Mechanical engineers serve as Design Engineers, Purchase, and Quality Control Executives, AME, aircraft designer, industrial machinery designer. Being termed as the mother branch of engineering, graduates from mechanical engineering enjoy a very broad spectrum of job opportunities from industries like refrigeration, aviation, shipping, aircraft design etc.

One can also choose to do a master's in Mechanical Engineering. Several other disciplines depend on mechanical engineering. Every such discipline will be there as a subject at the undergraduate level. If you are interested in any one of the sub-disciplines then you can opt for it at the master's level when you have completed your undergraduate degree in mechanical engineering. The sub-disciplines include Mechatronics, Thermodynamics, structural analysis, etc.

Mechanical engineering opens the way for a lot of job prospects and opportunities abroad. Mechanical engineers are in great demand all over the world like the aircraft industry, shipping industry, power plants or other major machinery giants.
