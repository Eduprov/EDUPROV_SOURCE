B.Tech/B.E Aerospace Engineering

Course Information

B.Tech/B.E Aerospace Engineering deals with unlike most traditional ground-based systems, optimality and reliability are of paramount importance in such systems. Aerospace Engineering is the discipline that is closest to what is popularly known as Rocket Science. The curriculum is very close to both Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering and equips the graduates to take up most jobs done by Mechanical Engineers. This includes the Automotive sector, Power sector, Manufacturing industry, R&D sectors, etc.

Aerospace Engineering as the name suggests deals with the secrete of the air. One who loves to fly and want to know about the secret of flying should surely consider this course before choosing one. Aerospace Engineering is a 4-year undergraduate degree program. Generally, entry-level aerospace engineering requires a bachelor's degree. The candidates who are interested in aerospace engineering should take courses in math, physics, and chemistry. This is an interesting and adventurous course.

The Bachelor's degree course includes laboratory, classroom, and field studies, like principles of general engineering, stability, control, propulsion, mechanics, structure and aerodynamics, which is a study of how an object moving in the air.

Eligibility

  • Bachelor’s Course: The basic requirement for B.E and B.Tech degree is 10+2 pass with Science (Chemistry, Physics, and Math) with the highest aggregate percentage marks.
  • The candidate must need to qualify for the IIT-JEE exam for admission into the Indian Institutes of Technology.
  • No need to worry about the entrance exams.
  • Guidance will be provided by the dedicated professionals of EDUPROV to crack the IIT-JEE both prelims and mains. We provide a solution for all your queries and will guide you until you get into the course.
  • Master’s Course: To get admission in M. Tech in Aerospace Engineering, a candidate should have a degree BE/B.Tech in Aerospace Engineering or equivalent.

Scope

After getting an undergraduate degree a well trained Aerospace engineer who knows how to the collaboration of engineering tools use and processes and who is well-known with robotics, modeling and simulation should have great opportunities. Aerospace engineers have the advantage to become technical specialists or supervise a team of technicians and engineers. Few of them become engineering managers and the remaining is move into executive positions like a program manager. An aerospace engineer who is trained in computational fluid dynamics software also has the employment opportunity, it allows companies to test designs in the digital environment at affordable costs.
