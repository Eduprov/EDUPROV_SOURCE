Bachelor Of Naturopathy & Yogic Science (BNYS)

Course Information

Naturopathy and Yogic science is a sort of prescribed medication which heals and support the patient in a philosophical way. It isn't kind of supplement, it works around the own recovery of the body like enhancing the immunity and detoxification of the whole body. Stressful life and imbalanced food diet in the current era lead to bad health so people are emphasizing in a natural way of healing.

Yoga is becoming more important in this era. In India, the awareness regarding yoga got improved and the government is taking more steps to make it effective. When it comes to the care and support of patient dealing with emotional misalignments, Bachelor of Naturopathy and Yogic Sciences (BNYS) is the academic course which can provide the skills to person. A Person can be well equipped and skilled to concentrate on the symptoms of the patient. In India, this is a course with 4.5 years of duration and then a compulsory one-year internship. A graduate in BNYS incurs the information on yoga therapy and practices. Below are the few key colleges which provide BNYS:

  • JSS Institute under M.G.R Medical University, Chennai
  • Government Naturopathic Medical College, Hyderabad
  • S.D.M College of Naturopathy and Yogic Sciences, Karnataka.

Eligibility

  • Completion of 12th.
  • A Student must possess science stream in 10+2.
  • A Student must possess 45% of marks in Physics, Chemistry & Biology subjects.

Subjects

In BNYS one will learn the principles of modern yoga therapy. The course makes one attain knowledge in drugless therapies such as meditation, Pranayama, etc. One will gain the knowledge to cure acute diseases. The duration of this course is 4 years and six months. In this course one will learn the basic medical subjects along with Philosophy of nature cure, Principle of yoga, Forensic medicine & toxicology, Psychology & basic psychiatry, Philosophy & practice of yoga, Naturopathic & modern diagnosis, Hospital management, Research methodology and recent advances, Obstetrics & Gynecology, Physical medicine and rehabilitation, and various yogic therapies. Apart from the course study, many universities offer the course in English along with an ancient language Sanskrit.

Scope

When it comes to health care, almost each and every area of the industry comes under such needs and support. Henceforth a BNYS doctor can have a broader scope of work in the below-mentioned category of the domain:

  • BNYS doctor can serve through the mission initiated by the Indian government, i.e. NRHM (National Rural Health Mission) and AYUSH
  • General practices in government and private hospitals.
  • General practice in clinics.
  • National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) under AYUSH, in all states of India
  • R&D sectors.
  • Practice as an Individual to provide care of individuals.
  • In Corporates as an expert on Stress Management.

One can have theirown clinic or work in private/ government hospitals as a general practitioner. Indian Government also offers work for the candidates who complete this course in NRHM. One can also work in specialty centers too. The option for higher studies is also available for this course. One can work as an instructor. The scope of a yoga instructor is tremendously high now. The opportunities for this course is very high in countries like USA, Canada, Australia due to the awareness about the wellness of Yoga over the past few years.
