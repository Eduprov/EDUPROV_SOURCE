Diploma in X-Ray Technology

A Radiology Technician or Radiology assistant is a paramedic technician who helps the patients and doctors in understanding and interpreting science of image production using MRI, CT Scans, X Rays, etc. in order to understand disorders, deformities, and illnesses of the human body in high detail. Radiography and medical technicians are one of the highly demanded jobs in the health care industry because it is a part of the routine and complex investigations. Radiology Technician or Radiology assistant help in producing images that are interpreted by radiologists and this aids in medical diagnosis.

Eligibility

  • To join the diploma in radiography and mental Imaging, students need to finish their 10th standard or 10+2 (PUC) level of education.
  • To join the B.Sc or Bachelors of Science in radiography and mental Imaging, students need to finish their 10+2 i.e. PUC level of education or have a diploma in allied health sciences or a diploma in paramedical sciences.
  • To join the M.Sc or Masters of Science in radiography and mental Imaging, students must have completed their B.Sc in radiography and mental Imaging or a B.Sc in any paramedic or allied health sciences.
  • Diploma in radiography and mental Imaging,
  • Bachelors of radiography and mental Imaging and
  • Masters of Science in radiography and mental Imaging

Scope

Some of the popular job options after the completion of this course include diagnostics, radiographist, radiation protection technician, X-Ray Technician, Logistics coordinator, Radiation therapist, MRI technician, Imaging technician, etc. The graduates can choose to study further or take up wonderful jobs in government hospitals, private hospitals, diagnostic centres, imaging labs, medical colleges, paramedical colleges, health centres, nursing homes, private and government care centres, Outpatient clinics, occupational health centres, polyclinics, medical equipment manufacturers and research organiZations , etc.
