Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery

Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery / BAMS is an integrated Indian medical undergraduate degree program that offers students a wonderful and useful mixture of both modern medicine and traditional knowledge of Ayurveda. Ayurveda is one of the ancient medical systems of the world. It traces its roots to the Vedic period. It is not only providing the cure but also prevent the recurrence of diseases. BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery) is an undergraduate degree program in old and ancient Ayurvedic medical system containing the prevention and cure to the body by increasing the harmony of Kapha, pitta, and Vata.

BAMS is one of the best courses after 12th for science students. The undergraduate medical degree is a four and a half year-long course and an extra one year of internship period program to get a live practical and real-life approach so that the students gain experience in reputed hospitals. The course has three sections, each for one and a half year-long period. These three sections are called as professionals or professional courses where the students will learn anatomy, physiology, Ayurveda and its long history, Sanskrit, toxicology, pharmacology both in allopathy and Ayurveda terms, surgery, ENT, skin, obstetrics, and gynecology. The graduates also learn about modern anatomy, principles of allopathic medicine, newer surgery methods, forensic medicine, botany, and preventive medicines. The student doctors graduate as a Vaidyar or Vr.

Eligibility

Here is the basic eligibility for students to join BAMS asprescribed by the government of India’s AYUSH department:

  • The students must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission Obam degree
  • The applicants must have taken physics, chemistry, and biology in 12th or PUC for their high school degree
  • The students must submit the NEET scores and apply for their state CET exams for admission through government quota seats

Scope

Newer trends have opened up many new careers and job opportunities for Ayurveda students. They can join as junior and senior doctors in government and private hospitals. Several chikitsa centers and Ayurveda clinics also recruit doctors. They can also choose to become professors in prestigious Ayurveda medical colleges. The students can also become health officers, pharmacologists, distributes, etc. If not, they can also open their own practice after the completion of BAMS. The job opportunities are limitless for students after this course.
